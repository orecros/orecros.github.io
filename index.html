Generating Surf Slopes and Platforms over Bezier Splines. [Sample Source]

These tools allow a level designer to quickly block out a level chunk, and can even allow a level to be generated by the computer at runtime.

Using bezier splines proved to be a challenge when it came to evenly spacing blocks (and slope segments) along the curve. Even within a single curve, a fractional amount along the parametric curve was not guaranteed to be the same length. Bezier curves, however, are much nicer for the designer to play with as it allows for less segments to cover the same path, and nice gradual curves with no noticeable cusps. The solution was to, upon generation of the curve or block pile, create a poly line along the bezier curve by connecting points fractionally placed along the curve. While these line segments weren't necessarily the same length, finding the length of each segment was trivial, as was lerping between two points to get a good approximation of the bezier curve.
This method worked great for the block placer, but the segments for the surf slope actually benefitted from being condensed at sharp corners, so it was changed to only use bezier splines.

Linked regions with poisson-disc sampling [Sample Source]

For this infinite runner, map chunks are generated one at a time ahead of the player. Each chunk is filled with platforms that should be homogenously spaced, even across adjacent chunks. Simply using poisson-disc sampling separately for each chunk leads to obvious gaps or seams between the chunks.

The solution was to, whenever blocks were to be generated, seed the generator with the positions of blocks from adjacent chunks. First, the positions of all the old blocks are converted to the same coordinate system as the generator. Blocks from old chunks that were not close enough to the new chunk to have any effect are culled from the list, and the remainder are added as seeds. seeds are tracked separately from newly generated blocks to prevent duplicate blocks from being generated.

In the figure displayed, 4 chunks are generated adjacent to each other and then populated with blocks in order. The green arrows are drawn between two blocks generated within the same chunk as they were generated by the algorithm. Red arrows are drawn across the gaps, with the starting point blocks being seeds in the new chunk.

Halloween Run - Game Design Document

This is a game design document I wrote. you can access it here. here's the introduction from the document:

Halloween-run is a first or third-person shooter for PC about using bizarre weapons and tools to survive the oncoming horde of halloween monsters. As the player survives, the procedurally generated neighborhood and its denizens slowly become more and more frightening: Trees grow faces. tombstones sprout up from empty lots. Eldritch tentacles sprout from the earth. Witches and werewolves are replaced by pale walking corpses and frozen giants. Will you live long enough to find the door back out to reality?

The image to the right gives a brief rundown of the procedural generation system. Most procedural generation is made to build an area a player will quickly run through once. My challenge in doing procedural generation for a rather small area that the player will play possibly multiple 20-minute games in is to create an area that can be learned and remembered but still be within the constraints of easy generation.

In addition to being procedurally generated, I plan to have the level slowly "corrupt" over the course of a game, giving birth to new stage hazards, new enemies, and a darker tone.

Tropical Zombie Survival - Garry's Mod Lua [Sample Source]

This is a fork of a popular, decade old garry's mod gamemode called Zombie Survival. I lead a team of 3 through 2 2-month development cycles, with 1 more planned cycle to be completed summer 2019.

In Broad Strokes, classic zombie survival was about utilizing scarce resources to survive 6 waves against the undead, mostly through running, hiding, barricading, and killing. Modern zombie survival has become bloated with legacy features to the point of it almost being unrecognizeable, with a heavier focus on team tactics and barricading.

Most key components of the game have been modified to combine the run-and-gun action of the old 2011-era Zombie Survival with some of the more modern developments of recent patches.
